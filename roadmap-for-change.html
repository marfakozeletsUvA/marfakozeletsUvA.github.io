UvA x Tapp Smart Architecture, 2025–2026

Roadmap for Change

Agent-based model identifying unsafe cycling infrastructure in Amsterdam before accidents happen.

ABM Mesa GeoPandas sensor data urban mobility
the problem

Amsterdam identifies dangerous infrastructure only after harm occurs. Near-misses go unrecorded. The model detects risk before it becomes an accident.

270
cyclist deaths across the Netherlands, 2023
2,507
ambulance calls in Amsterdam, 2022
approach
01
Sensor data
Bike-mounted sensors collected GPS and acceleration every second across 110 trips through Amsterdam. 718,303 total readings.
02
ABM simulation
Each trip becomes an agent navigating the Amsterdam road network. Hard braking (below -2.0g) triggers probabilistic state transitions: SAFE to AT RISK to UNSAFE.
03
Infrastructure overlay
Braking events mapped against traffic lights, road surface type, and facility type to isolate infrastructure-driven risk from behavioural noise.
key findings
93.9%
of hard braking occurs away from traffic lights
55.5%
of simulated trips ended in an UNSAFE state
7x
more braking events on unsafe vs safe trips (730 vs 97)
3-4x
more braking per km on brick and tile vs asphalt
